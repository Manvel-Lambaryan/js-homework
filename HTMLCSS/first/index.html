What is Semantic HTML?

Semantic HTML refers to the use of HTML markup that accurately represents the purpose of the content it contains. Instead of using generic tags like `

Benefits for Developers and Users

For developers, semantic HTML creates a clear, logical structure that is easier to maintain and debug. When a new developer joins a project, they can quickly grasp the layout and purpose of different parts of the page. For users, especially those with disabilities who rely on assistive technologies, semantic tags provide a robust navigation framework. A screen reader can announce the presence of a `

` or `
` element, allowing the user to skip to a specific section without having to listen to the entire page content.

Common Semantic Tags

  • <header>: Defines introductory content, typically a group of introductory or navigational aids.
  • <nav>: Contains major navigation links for the site.
  • <main>: Represents the dominant content of the `` of a document.
  • <article>: Encloses a self-contained composition, such as a blog post, a forum post, or a news article.
  • <section>: Groups related content that forms a coherent part of a document.
  • <aside>: Contains content that is tangentially related to the content around it, often a sidebar.
  • <footer>: Represents the footer for its nearest sectioning content or body, typically containing information about the author, copyright data, or related documents.
